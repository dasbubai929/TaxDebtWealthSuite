In Canada, Registered Retirement Savings Plans (RRSPs) are one of the most effective vehicles for building pre-tax retirement wealth. However, as you approach retirement, Canadian tax law mandates that all RRSPs must be closed or converted into an income stream by the end of the year you turn **age 71**. Most Canadians choose to convert their RRSPs into a **Registered Retirement Income Fund (RRIF)** to satisfy statutory minimum annual withdrawal requirements.

## 1. RRSP to RRIF Conversion Milestone (Age 71)

By December 31 of the calendar year in which you reach **age 71**, you must choose one of three options for your RRSP funds:
1. **Convert the RRSP into a RRIF** (the most popular tax-deferred choice).
2. **Purchase an eligible Life Annuity** from an insurance company.
3. **Deregister and withdraw the full RRSP balance as cash** (subject to immediate, heavy withholding taxes and marginal income tax spikes).

Once converted into a RRIF, you can no longer make new contributions to the account. Mandatory annual minimum withdrawals begin in the calendar year following conversion (at **age 72**).

## 2. CRA RRIF Minimum Withdrawal Percentage Table

The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) mandates a statutory minimum percentage that must be withdrawn from your RRIF each year, calculated as a percentage of your account value on January 1.

### Formula for Ages Under 71
If you convert your RRSP to a RRIF before age 71, your minimum annual withdrawal percentage is calculated using the formula:

$$\text{Minimum Withdrawal \%} = \frac{1}{90 - \text{Age on Jan 1}}$$

| Age on Jan 1 | Prescribed Minimum % | Mandatory Payout (C$400,000 Balance) |

| **72** | **5.40%** | C$21,600.00 |

| **95+** | **20.00%** | C$80,000.00 |

## 3. Canadian Withholding Taxes on RRIF Withdrawals

- **Mandatory Minimum Amount:** The statutory minimum RRIF withdrawal is **exempt from CRA withholding tax at source** (though it is included in your taxable income at tax time).
- **Withdrawals in Excess of Minimum:** Any amount withdrawn above the mandatory minimum is subject to immediate CRA withholding tax at source:
  - Up to C$5,000 excess: **10%** withholding tax (20% in Quebec).
  - C$5,001 to C$15,000 excess: **20%** withholding tax (25% in Quebec).
  - Over C$15,000 excess: **30%** withholding tax (30% in Quebec).

---

## 4. RRIF Income Splitting and Younger Spouse Electing Strategy

- **Spousal Age Election:** When opening your RRIF, you can elect to base your mandatory minimum withdrawal percentages on the **age of your younger spouse**. Because the younger spouse's age yields a lower percentage factor, this strategy reduces mandatory RRIF drawdowns, helping preserve capital and prevent OAS income clawbacks.
- **Pension Income Splitting:** At age 65 or older, RRIF income qualifies for Canadian Pension Income Splitting, allowing you to allocate up to **50% of your eligible RRIF income** to a lower-earning spouse.
