The Australian Superannuation system requires APRA-regulated funds and Self-Managed Super Funds (SMSFs) to enforce statutory minimum annual drawdown limits once a member transfers their accumulation account into an **Account-Based Pension (ABP)** or **Transition to Retirement (TTR)** pension phase. Knowing how minimum drawdown percentage brackets scale with age ensures compliance with Australian Taxation Office (ATO) standards while maximizing tax-free income.

## 1. Conditions of Release & Pension Phase Options

Before accessing superannuation in Australia, you must satisfy a statutory **Condition of Release**:
1. **Reaching Preservation Age (60) & Retiring:** Once you reach age 60 and cease employment, you can access your super uncapped.
2. **Reaching Age 65:** You gain full, unrestricted access to your super regardless of whether you have retired from the workforce.

When transitioning your super into the pension phase, you move funds into an **Account-Based Pension**. Account balances in the pension phase enjoy a **0% tax rate on investment earnings and capital gains** within the super fund (up to the Transfer Balance Cap limit of A$1.9 million).

---

## 2. ATO Statutory Minimum Pension Payment Standards

To maintain the tax-free status of investment earnings inside an Account-Based Pension, fund members must draw down at least a statutory minimum pension payment each financial year. The minimum payment is calculated by multiplying your pension account balance on **July 1** (or starting balance if commenced during the year) by the statutory percentage for your age bracket:

| Age Bracket on July 1 | ATO Minimum Drawdown % | Mandatory Annual Payment (A$500,000 Balance) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Under 65** | **4.0%** | A$20,000.00 |
| **65 – 74** | **5.0%** | A$25,000.00 |
| **75 – 79** | **6.0%** | A$30,000.00 |
| **80 – 84** | **7.0%** | A$35,000.00 |
| **85 – 89** | **9.0%** | A$45,000.00 |
| **90 – 94** | **11.0%** | A$55,000.00 |
| **95+** | **14.0%** | A$70,000.00 |

```
Calculation Example (Age 68, Balance A$500,000):
• Applicable Age Bracket: 65–74 (5.0%)
• Minimum Annual Pension Payment: 5.0% × A$500,000 = A$25,000 per year
• Minimum Fortnightly Payout: A$25,000 / 26 = A$961.54 per fortnight
```

---

## 3. Tax Treatment of Super Drawdowns (Ages 60+)

For individuals aged **60 or older**, superannuation drawdown payments taken from an Account-Based Pension are **100% tax-free** in your hands. Pension payments do not need to be declared on your personal Australian tax return as assessable income.

### Transition to Retirement (TTR) Pensions
If you reach your preservation age (60) but wish to keep working, a **Transition to Retirement (TTR)** pension allows you to draw down between a minimum of **4%** and a maximum of **10%** of your balance per year. TTR pensions allow you to supplement income while reducing working hours or making tax-deductible salary sacrifice contributions.

---

## 4. Consequences of Failing the Minimum Drawdown

If an APRA fund or SMSF fails to pay the mandatory minimum drawdown amount by **June 30**, the account loses its tax-exempt pension status for the entire financial year. Earnings inside the fund will be taxed at the standard superannuation accumulation rate of **15%**.
