Managing pre-tax retirement accounts like Traditional IRAs, 401(k)s, 403(b)s, and 457(b) plans in the United States requires navigating the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) **Required Minimum Distribution (RMD)** framework. Failure to take mandatory annual RMDs triggers severe tax penalties, while taking uncoordinated withdrawals can push you into higher federal income tax brackets.

## 1. What are Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)?

An RMD is the statutory minimum amount you must withdraw from your tax-deferred retirement accounts each year once you reach a specific milestone age. Because contributions to Traditional IRAs and 401(k)s were made with pre-tax dollars, the IRS mandates distributions so that tax revenues can be collected during your retirement lifetime.

### SECURE Act 2.0 RMD Age Timelines
Legislative changes introduced by SECURE Act 2.0 updated RMD starting ages based on your birth year:
- **Born before 1951:** RMDs began at age **70½** or **72**.
- **Born 1951 through 1959:** RMDs begin at age **73**.
- **Born 1960 or later:** RMDs begin at age **75** (effective starting in 2033).

```
RMD Calculation Formula:
Required Minimum Distribution = ( Account Balance as of December 31 of Prior Year ) / ( IRS Distribution Period Factor )
```

## 3. Account Specific Rules: Traditional vs. Roth IRAs

- **Traditional IRAs & 401(k)s:** Mandatory RMDs apply every year starting at age 73/75. All withdrawals are taxed as ordinary federal and state income.
- **Roth IRAs:** Original account owners are **completely exempt** from mandatory RMDs during their lifetime. Funds inside a Roth IRA continue to compound tax-free indefinitely.
- **Employer 401(k) Still-Working Exception:** If you are still working past age 73 and own less than 5% of the company, you can defer RMDs from your current employer's 401(k) until you actually retire.

---

## 4. Penalty Rules for Missed RMDs

Under previous law, failing to withdraw the full RMD amount resulted in a crushing **50% excise tax** on the shortfall. Under SECURE Act 2.0, the penalty has been reduced to **25%** (and further reduced to **10%** if corrected in a timely manner via IRS Form 5329).

### Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCDs)
To satisfy your RMD without increasing your Adjusted Gross Income (AGI), individuals age 70½ or older can execute a **Qualified Charitable Distribution (QCD)**. You can transfer up to **$105,000 per year** directly from an IRA to a qualified 501(c)(3) charity. The transfer counts toward your RMD but is excluded from taxable income.
