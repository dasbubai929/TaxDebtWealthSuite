When homeowners in the United States receive a financial windfall—such as an inheritance, annual work bonus, or proceeds from selling an asset or previous home—they often wonder how to use that capital to reduce monthly living expenses. While refinancing is a common strategy, a **mortgage recast** offers a faster, cheaper alternative to lower monthly payments without changing interest rates or incurring closing costs.

## 1. What is a Mortgage Recast?

A mortgage recast (also known as loan re-amortization) is an option offered by most US mortgage servicers. When you execute a recast, you make a lump-sum principal payment (typically $5,000 to $10,000 or more) toward your existing mortgage balance. In response, your lender recalculates your monthly principal and interest payment based on the lower balance over the remaining term of the loan.

### Key Characteristics of a Recast
- **Interest Rate Unchanged:** Your existing interest rate remains exactly the same.
- **Loan Term Unchanged:** If you have 22 years remaining on a 30-year mortgage, your remaining term stays 22 years.
- **No Credit Check or Appraisal:** Unlike refinancing, recasting requires no income verification, employment checks, or home appraisal.
- **Low Fee:** Servicers usually charge a modest administrative processing fee between **$200 and $500**.

```
Example Recast Scenario ($350,000 Balance at 6.5% Interest, 25 Years Remaining):
• Original Monthly Payment: $2,362.81 / month
• Lump-Sum Recast Payment:  $50,000
• New Loan Principal:        $300,000
• Recast Processing Fee:    $250
--------------------------------------------------
• New Monthly Payment:       $2,025.27 / month
• Monthly Cash Savings:      $337.54 / month (-14.3% Reduction)
• Net Interest Saved:        $51,012.00 over remaining 25 years
```

## 2. Mortgage Recast vs. Refinancing

| Feature | Mortgage Recast | Mortgage Refinancing |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Goal** | Lower monthly payment with lump sum | Lower interest rate or change term |
| **Upfront Cost** | $200 – $500 flat fee | 2% – 5% of loan amount in closing costs |
| **Credit & Income Check** | Not required | Required (Full underwriting) |
| **Home Appraisal** | Not required | Usually required ($500 – $800) |
| **Interest Rate Impact** | Retains existing rate | Replaces with current market rate |
| **Ideal Market Condition** | Existing rate is lower than current market rates | Current market rates are lower than existing rate |

## 3. Eligibility & Lender Requirements (Fannie Mae & Freddie Mac)

Most conventional fixed-rate loans backed by Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac are eligible for recasting, provided you satisfy basic servicer conditions:

1. **Minimum Lump Sum Requirement:** Lenders typically require a minimum lump-sum principal payment of at least **$5,000 to $10,000** (or 10% of the remaining balance).
2. **Account Standing:** Your mortgage must be current with no history of delinquent or missed payments in the past 12 months.
3. **Eligible Loan Types:** Conventional fixed-rate mortgages are almost universally eligible. FHA and VA loans are generally **ineligible** for recasting under government program guidelines, while USDA loans have restricted availability.
4. **Waiting Period:** Some servicers require you to have made a minimum number of regular monthly payments (e.g., 6 consecutive months) before requesting a recast.

---

## 4. How to Request a Mortgage Recast Step-by-Step

1. **Contact Your Mortgage Servicer:** Call your lender's customer service department and inquire specifically about their "Mortgage Recast" or "Re-amortization" policy.
2. **Confirm Fees & Requirements:** Ask for their written recast application form, minimum lump-sum threshold, processing fee amount, and instructions.
3. **Submit Application & Lump Sum:** Send the completed recast agreement along with your certified funds or check for the lump sum plus processing fee.
4. **Sign & Return Recast Agreement:** Your servicer will issue a modified payment schedule reflecting your new, lower monthly installment starting within 1 to 2 billing cycles.
