A high salary does not make you wealthy; it simply gives you the potential to become wealthy. If you earn $300,000 a year but spend $300,000 a year, you are financially identical to someone who earns $50,000 and spends $50,000. 

To shift from income-generation to true wealth-building, you must transition to a balance-sheet mentality. As outlined in our **[How to Build Wealth](/blog/how-to-build-wealth/)** hub, the only metric that accurately reflects your financial health is your **Net Worth**.

## The Formula

The calculation is deceptively simple:
**Total Assets - Total Liabilities = Net Worth**

### 1. Total Assets (What You Own)
List everything of significant value that you own. 
- **Liquid Assets:** Checking accounts, savings accounts, money market funds.
- **Investments:** Brokerage accounts, 401(k)s, IRAs, crypto, equity in private businesses.
- **Real Estate:** The current market value of your primary residence and any rental properties.

### 2. Total Liabilities (What You Owe)
List every outstanding debt.
- **Consumer Debt:** Credit card balances, auto loans, personal loans.
- **Student Loans:** All outstanding educational debt.
- **Mortgages:** The remaining principal balance on your home and investment properties.

## The Importance of the Trendline

Your net worth on any given day is less important than the trajectory of the trendline over time. 

By calculating your net worth on the 1st of every month, you create a feedback loop. When you pay down debt, your liabilities decrease, pushing your net worth up. When the stock market rallies, your assets increase, pushing your net worth up. 

If your net worth is stagnant while your income is rising, you have a definitive data point indicating severe lifestyle inflation. You must immediately divert cash flow away from consumption and toward asset acquisition or debt reduction.

## Tracking Tools

While there are dozens of automated apps (like Empower or Monarch Money) that can sync to your bank accounts, many high-net-worth individuals prefer the control and privacy of a custom Excel or Google Sheets spreadsheet. A manual spreadsheet forces you to engage with the numbers monthly, reinforcing the psychological shift required to build lasting wealth.
