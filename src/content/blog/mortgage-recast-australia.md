Australian homeowners holding variable or fixed-rate home loans often ask how to deploy lump-sum funds to lower their monthly mortgage repayments. While the term "mortgage recast" is specific to US lending, Australia's banking system offers even more flexible structures—such as **100% Mortgage Offset Accounts** and **Redraw Facilities**—overseen by the Australian Prudential Regulation Authority (APRA) and ASIC Moneysmart.

## 1. Mortgage Recasting vs. Australian Home Loan Tools

In Australia, formal loan recasting is rarely necessary because Australian banks (Big Four: CBA, Westpac, NAB, ANZ) provide dynamic liquidity tools built directly into variable home loans:

1. **Mortgage Offset Account:** A separate transaction account linked to your home loan. Money sitting in the offset account is subtracted from your loan principal before daily interest is calculated.
2. **Redraw Facility:** Allows you to make extra principal prepayments directly into your home loan account and withdraw ("redraw") those extra funds later if needed.
3. **Formal Repayment Recalculation (Recast):** If you make a permanent, non-redrawable extra payment, you can instruct your lender to recalculate your minimum monthly repayment over your remaining loan term.

```
Comparison Example (A$500,000 Balance at 6.0% Interest, 25 Years Remaining):
• Original Monthly Repayment: A$3,221.51 / month
• Lump-Sum Principal Payment: A$50,000
• Recalculated Loan Principal: A$450,000
--------------------------------------------------
• New Monthly Repayment:       A$2,899.36 / month
• Monthly Repayment Savings:   A$322.15 / month (-10.0% Reduction)
```

## 2. Offset Account vs. Lump-Sum Principal Reduction

| Parameter | Offset Account Strategy | Lump-Sum Principal Recast |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fund Liquidity** | 100% Liquid (Instant ATM/EFTPOS access) | Funds permanently applied to loan |
| **Interest Reduction** | Reduces interest daily | Reduces interest daily |
| **Monthly Payment Impact** | Minimum payment stays same (faster equity) | Minimum payment recalculated lower |
| **Tax Impact for Investors** | Preserves tax deductibility if property becomes investment | Permanently reduces deductible debt balance |

---

## 3. How to Recalculate Your Repayments with Major Australian Banks

If your primary goal is to lower your required fortnightly or monthly cash outlay, contact your Australian lender after making an extra payment:
- **Variable Loans:** Most Australian banks allow you to request a repayment recalculation online or over the phone for free.
- **Fixed Loans:** Fixed-rate home loans in Australia usually restrict extra prepayments (typically capped at A$10,000 per year). Exceeding this cap triggers Break Costs. Wait until your fixed rate expires before applying large lump sums.
